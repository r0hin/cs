Section 1 Problem & Solution


Here at UCC, students regularly attending the S.A.S. for fitness are strongly inconvenienced by the old methods of recording their progress and data. Similarly, it is very unorganized and unintuitive at the moment for their advisor to view the students' data and recognize trends in the behavior. There are many issues to this solution such as the fact that papers recorded are easily lost and hard to manage. Additionally, when participating in athletics, a possible risk is writing messily.

The solution requested is to make an online platform accessible to all students with their laptops so that they can easily record and update their data and quickly receive feedback or have a custom graph created for them. The solution will allow students to quickly and intuitively track their S.A.S. Progress in a web interface, subsequently diminishing the previous issues with using pen and paper. This will also allow the supervisor to quickly view a student's data and allow for them to make more meaningful conclusions and teachings.

The solution will be for a certain and particular demographic. From the existing users who attend the S.A.S, for recording their habits and workflows, they will be using this solution to do it easier and more unituitively. The audience is already there and mainly consists of Upper Canada College students from year 8 up to year 12 who regularly attend the S.A.S fitness center. The client is Luke Buttery (Mr. Buttery), who is looking for a solution to the inconveniences outlined above.

Section 4 Design Brief

Fitness is one of the most important parts of one's life. It leads to countless health benifits but some people are restricted by the lack of innovation there. Less than 5% of adults participate in 30 minutes of physical activity each day so making sure that it becomes a habit while you're young is very important. The fitness centers at schools are very useful in combatting this, however, there will always be setbacks or issues with parts of the method. For example, a local problem set in the S.A.S Fitness Center at Upper Canada College in Ontario currently in use by many students from year 8 to year 12, is that the students are having a difficult time recording, logging and managing their progress. It is currently done on a single sheet of paper which can be improved vastly. The product is a web based interface accessible to anyone with their phones or laptops by going to the website. The solution will be built with material design in mind and resemble popular products today in order to create a meaningful solution to this problem. Firebase, HTML, CSS and Javascript will be essential towards making this project a reality and include lots of functions to make the site extremely useful. The product's build phase will take place over the next few months, after completing the previous design cycle elements. The research required for the product was mostly about Firebase and the product will Firebase Authentication and Firebase Firestore. The solution's use will be in use from when it is done to whenever a better solution is present which could be in a 10 months or 10 years.
